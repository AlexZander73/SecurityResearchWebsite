In plain words

This page explains one common AI risk in plain terms and shows a safer default you can apply quickly.

What this risk looks like

Attackers can produce convincing phishing text, fake support chats, and personalized lures at scale with minimal cost.

What can go wrong

  • More polished phishing that bypasses instinctive filters
  • Rapid iteration of scam variants
  • Targeted personalization from public data

Safer patterns

  1. Teach verification-first habits, not style-based trust.
  2. Implement secure communication channels for sensitive requests.
  3. Use behavioral detection for scam operations.

Minimum control set

  • Internal anti-impersonation playbooks
  • Payment and account-change verification controls
  • User education with realistic scam examples
AI builder reminder: Model output is not policy. Every sensitive action needs explicit guardrails.
