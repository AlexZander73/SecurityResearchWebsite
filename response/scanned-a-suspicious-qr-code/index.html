Stay calm first

A practical, calm checklist for what to do immediately after scanning an unknown QR destination.

What the risk depends on

Risk is low if you only scanned and closed the page. Risk increases if you entered credentials, payment details, installed an app, or approved a prompt.

Immediate steps

  1. Close the page and do not interact further.
  2. Review browser downloads and remove unknown files.
  3. If you entered credentials, reset that password from a known-good link.
  4. If you entered payment info, contact your card provider.

Priority order

  1. Contain: stop interaction and disconnect risky app installs.
  2. Secure: rotate exposed credentials and sessions.
  3. Financial: lock cards or monitor for fraudulent payments.
  4. Record: keep screenshots/URLs for reporting.

Who to contact

Contact your bank if payment data was shared, your employer IT team if a work account was involved, and the venue where the QR code appeared so they can remove it.

What to monitor

  • Unknown login alerts
  • New payment authorizations
  • Password reset emails you did not request
  • Unexpected app permissions or mobile profile installs

Prevention for next time

Use trusted apps or bookmarked sites for payments and account logins. Treat QR scans as untrusted links unless you can verify destination context.

Important: Fast containment beats perfect certainty. Start with reversible safety steps now.
